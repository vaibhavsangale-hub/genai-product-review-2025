Executive summary

Key observation: the learner mix is heavily non-tech and largely freshers, but there is a meaningful working professional segment. The current curriculum strongly emphasizes text/visual/audio creation, while working professionals are asking for operational automation and productivity outcomes.

What’s driving the product pressure:

  • Mismatch: working professionals want “AI at work” (automation, reporting, productivity) more than text/audio/video creation.
  • Brochure perception: tools count reduced from 30 tools (older pitch) to 12 tools (current brochure), reducing perceived value.
  • Price expectation: at ₹28,000, learners expect visible, structured career outcomes and stronger career assistance packaging.

Recommended change-set (not a full rewrite)

These changes keep the 90 hours structure, but rebalance “what learners feel” and “what working professionals need” without removing GenAI fundamentals.

1) Reposition the program outcome (single-line outcome)

Outcome suggestion: “Learners can use GenAI to automate day-to-day work, build simple AI workflows and assistants without coding, and present a portfolio (resume + LinkedIn + capstone) aligned to real roles.”

2) Curriculum rebalance (what to decrease vs increase)

  • Decrease time spent purely on text/audio/video creation demos; keep only what is needed to build “AI at work” competency.
  • Increase operational automation: Make.com (and/or alternatives), AI agents for tasks, and “workflow thinking” using real office scenarios (reports, follow-ups, CRM notes, meeting summaries, email drafts, SOP creation).
  • Add 1–2 short modules (or sub-modules) focused on: AI for data analysis (Excel/Sheets + dashboards), market research outputs, and project management support.

3) Brochure/tooling fix (conversion lever)

  • Replace “12 tools” framing with “Core toolkit + demo toolkit”: show 12 core tools taught deeply, plus 20+ tools demonstrated across use-cases (so the brochure perception returns closer to the earlier 30 tools promise without inflating teaching time).
  • Make “no-code automation” and “AI at work” the hero sections on the brochure, not just multimodal creation.

4) Career assistance packaging (expectation at ₹28,000)

  • Define a visible career deliverable pack: resume + LinkedIn + 1 capstone narrative + portfolio repository, with clear checkpoints and review rubrics.
  • Position Career Readiness as outcomes (deliverables), not sessions (hours).
Notes: This report intentionally avoids prescribing a brand-new curriculum. It identifies the smallest set of shifts required to (a) match the 30% working professional segment’s expectations, and (b) increase perceived value for the 70% fresher base.
